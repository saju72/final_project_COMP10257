About Neon Tetra

Neon Tetra is a freshwater fish of the tetra fish family.It's scientific name is Paracheirodon innesi. Neon Tetras have vibrant blue coloured bodies with striking red markings from the belly to the tail. This type of fishes are found in the northern and western Amazon basins in Peru, Brazil, and Colombia. They needs less care thats why Neon Tetras are popular among the begginer fishkeepers. Neon Tetras are one of the smallest species of aquarium fish. Size of this fish varies between 1 and 1.5 inches. Neon Tetras have the lifespan of up to eight years. There are over 150 different types of neon tetras such as black neon tetras and diamond head neon tetras.

Tank Requirements:

  1. WaterType: Water should be fresh and hard.
  2. TankSize: Tank must have minimum 10 gallons, and one extra gallon of water for every additional fish.

World Count

Neon tetras are a schooling species, so you should keep a minimum of six to ten neon tetras together in one tank. Neon tetras will feel uncomfortable, get stressed, and maybe even die if you keep too few of them together.There is an estimate of 1.8 million Neon tetras being bred every single month after being collected from their wild habitat in Colombia, Peru, and Brazil and then imported to United states and to other continents.
